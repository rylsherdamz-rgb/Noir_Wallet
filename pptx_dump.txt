--- ppt/slides/slide6.xml ---
How It Works
One tap sets off the whole pipeline — the user never sees any of it.
1 · Tap   
Terminal reads the tag UID over NFC/RFID
2 · Hash &amp; Resolve   
UID is SHA-256 hashed; device_registry resolves the wallet
3 · Debit the Agent   
The device's x402 agent wallet signs within its daily limit
4 · Settle &amp; Cash Out   
Stellar finalizes in 3–5s; PDAX bridges to PHP
The gateway returns 202 Accepted instantly and settles asynchronously — 
under two seconds from tap to “Paid”
 — with an offline queue that batches transactions when the network drops.
Noir Wallet
--- ppt/slides/slide7.xml ---
System Architecture
Three Core Layers
device_registry · Soroban
Hardware-to-wallet mapping with terminal whitelisting and per-device replay protection on-chain
Payment Gateway · Rust + actix-web
Async payment queue, fee channels, daily-limit enforcement, and idempotent transaction records in PostgreSQL
PDAX Fiat Bridge
Licensed Philippine exchange integration — auto-liquidation webhooks convert merchant USDC to PHP
Only SHA-256 device hashes touch the chain. 
Raw card UIDs never leave the terminal, and no custodial server ever holds user funds.
Noir Wallet
--- ppt/slides/slide8.xml ---
🔒
DEFENSE IN DEPTH
Security-First Design
✓
Hashed Device Identity
Tag UIDs are SHA-256 hashed at the terminal — the raw UID is never stored or transmitted
✓
Terminal Authentication
Only whitelisted POS terminals can initiate payments, via API keys and signed challenges
✓
Replay Protection
Every tap carries a signed nonce; 500ms anti-double-tap rate limiting per device
✓
Blast-Radius Limits
Per-device daily limits and isolated x402 agent wallets — a lost tag never exposes main funds
✓
Encrypted Key Custody
Fee-channel and agent keys sealed with envelope encryption (KMS-backed KeyManager)

THE HOOK
So Why Can’t I Buy Lunch With Crypto?
Everyone says crypto is the future of payments. So why can’t I buy lunch with it? To spend crypto today, I need a cold wallet in my pocket or a phone in my hand — unlock, open, sign, confirm. If paying takes this much effort, people simply won’t use it.
Noir Wallet
--- ppt/slides/slide13.xml ---
THE PRINCIPLE
Effortless Is What Defines the Future
The future of payments isn’t defined by the technology behind them — it’s defined by how effortless they are to use. Every generation of payment systems has removed friction: from cash, to cards, to contactless tap-to-pay. The next step is making blockchain payments just as seamless.
From research to vision: 
during senior high school, for our Research 2 subject, I conducted a study on the acceptability of decentralized applications using the Technology Acceptance Model (TAM) as our theoretical framework. Our findings reinforced a simple idea: when technology is easier to use and requires less effort, people are far more likely to adopt it. That insight shaped the vision behind Noir Wallet.
Noir Wallet

THE SOLUTION
Introducing Noir Wallet
Noir Wallet brings the convenience of tap-to-pay to blockchain. Simply tap an NFC card, sticker, or wearable — and the payment is complete. 
No app. No unlock. No confirmation.
 Our x402 protocol authorizes the payment the moment your NFC tag touches the terminal, while Stellar settles the transaction in under two seconds for a fraction of a cent.
Noir Wallet
--- ppt/slides/slide15.xml ---
TRUST &amp; SECURITY
Simple to Use. Robust by Design.
The experience is simple, but the security is robust. Every NFC device is linked to its own agent wallet with a configurable daily spending limit. If it’s lost, you can revoke it instantly, and the remaining funds are automatically returned to your main wallet. Device identities are hashed on-chain through Soroban, terminals are whitelisted, and every transaction is protected against replay attacks.
